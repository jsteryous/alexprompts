FACT CHECK: every specific figure (numbers, dates, study findings, geographies, series ids) must trace to the brief; flag or cut anything that does not; do not add facts. A statistic with no source in the brief is a fact error.

INVENT NOTHING: every concrete detail, including any scene, anecdote, person, place description, dialogue, or local color, must trace to the brief or a cited source. Flag and cut anything invented for flavor, especially in the opening. A fabricated anecdote is as serious as a fabricated number. VOICE AND POV: confirm the piece reads as objective third-person research, with NO first-person narration (no "I," "we," "my street") and no vlog framing. HONESTY DISCIPLINE (the core of this engine, hunt it hard):

Every apparent effect must sit next to its confounder. If the draft states the obvious answer without the catch from the brief's CONFOUNDERS section, send it back.
- CONFIDENCE MUST MATCH THE DATA. A figure the brief marks CONTESTED, UNKNOWN, or secondhand must be spoken as such, not as settled fact. A strong claim needs strong data behind it in the brief.
- CAVEATS PRESENT. Every load-bearing number carries its caveat in natural speech (margin of error, vendor model, single-city study, repeat-sales vs median price). A bare number is how the old engine got burned.

QUOTES: every quotation must be VERBATIM from the brief's KEY QUOTES, word for word, correct speaker. If the words inside the marks are not in the brief exactly, fix them to the verbatim text or strip the marks and leave it as a paraphrase. A misquote is a fact error.

TONE: confirm the piece lands on a useful, grounded read, not on fear. No doom headline, no "you are exposed" framing. Confirm it steelmans the opposing view before resolving, and that it follows the data even when the data deflates a popular belief.
